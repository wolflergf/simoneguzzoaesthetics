Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to your questions about aesthetic procedures, safety, and aftercare. Information based on clinical experience and scientific evidence.

Botox is one of the safest aesthetic procedures when performed by qualified professionals. As a specialist nurse, I follow strict safety protocols and only use products approved by the MHRA (UK).

Side effects are rare and usually mild, and may include:

  • Slight redness at the injection site
  • Minor bruising (rare)
  • Mild headache (temporary)

All risks are discussed during the consultation, and you will receive full guidance on post-procedure care.

Botox results typically last between 4 to 6 months, varying according to:

  • Individual metabolism
  • Treated area
  • Amount of product used
  • Frequency of previous treatments

With regular applications, many clients notice that the results tend to last longer, as the muscles become 'used to' the relaxation.

The discomfort is minimal. Most clients describe the feeling as a 'mosquito bite'. I use ultrafine needles and techniques that minimize any discomfort.

For more sensitive clients, I can apply topical anesthetic before the procedure. The whole process only takes 10-15 minutes.

After the procedure, you may resume your normal activities immediately.

Post-procedure care is simple and you'll receive detailed instructions:

First 24 hours:

  • Avoid intense exercise
  • Don't massage the treated area
  • Avoid excessive sun exposure
  • Don't lie face down (for Botox)

First 7 days:

  • Avoid saunas and facial treatments
  • Use sun protection
  • Apply ice if swelling occurs

I'll be available to answer any questions and monitor your recovery.

Timing varies depending on the type of procedure:

Botox:

  • First signs: 3-5 days
  • Full result: 10-14 days
  • Maximum effect: 2-3 weeks

Dermal Fillers:

  • Immediate result (with initial swelling)
  • True result: 7-14 days
  • Final result: 4-6 weeks

I schedule a follow-up appointment after 2 weeks to assess results and make adjustments if necessary.

Yes, certain conditions may contraindicate the procedures:

Absolute contraindications:

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding
  • Known allergy to components
  • Active infection in the treatment area
  • Neuromuscular disorders (for Botox)

Relative contraindications:

  • Use of anticoagulants
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • History of keloid scarring

During the consultation, we review your complete medical history to ensure the procedure is safe for you.
